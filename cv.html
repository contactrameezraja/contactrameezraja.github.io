About Me

I started my career in 2014 as a Business Analyst at DLL Group, where there were no AI tools, no automation frameworks, and no shortcuts. Just spreadsheets, raw data, and a need to figure things out. I found it frustrating early on when I'd curiously ask questions about why something was done a certain way and the answer was always "we've always done it this way." That didn't sit right with me. Within a year I'd identified a quarter of a million pounds in cost savings through pure analytical focus and was awarded Employee of the Year, the youngest person to ever receive it. Usually it was the sales people who won those awards, but I was just a kid fresh out of university working in operations in the back office, and I won. That was a turning point for me. I even picked up the nickname "the automation kid" because I couldn't stop finding ways to make things more efficient.

That experience shaped how I approach data to this day. I moved to Citi, where over five years I worked across Private Banking in New York, Legal Markets in Belfast, and Global People Analytics in London. Each role got more technical. I went from building Power BI dashboards to designing Snowflake data lakes, automating regulatory reporting pipelines, and implementing ML algorithms for contract analysis with PwC. Along the way I picked up an Innovation Award, three Gold Gratitude Awards, and learned that the best data work isn't just about the engineering, it's about understanding why the business needs it in the first place. I quickly realised that no matter where I am in the world, the same problems exist: data silos, absence of documentation, bureaucracy, and people accepting broken processes as normal. It made me realise what type of data champion I am. When I touch something, I own it. It becomes my work, my responsibility, and I don't leave until it's right.

What drives me is simple. I like solving problems that people have accepted as "just the way things are." The manual report that takes 40 hours a week. The question nobody can answer because the knowledge lives in one person's head. The pipeline that fails silently overnight. Those are the problems I enjoy fixing, and I've spent 12 years building the technical toolkit and business understanding to do it well. I'm currently open to Data Engineer and Data Analyst roles across London, Belfast, and Edinburgh. If you think I'd be a good fit, I'd love to hear from you.
